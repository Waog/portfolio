Below we explain how personal data is processed when you visit this website and when you contact us by email or telephone.

Controller

The controller for data processing on this website is Oliver Stadie IT GmbH, Memhardstr. 7, 10178 Berlin, Germany. You can contact us by email at info@oliverstadie.com or by telephone at +49 1520 2825986.

Accessing the website

The website is technically provided through GitHub Pages. When the website is accessed, GitHub processes in particular the IP address of the requesting device and other technical request and usage data to the extent required for delivery and security. This processing serves the provision of the website and the defence against security risks on the basis of Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.

We also use Cloudflare for DNS resolution, proxying, content delivery, network and attack protection, as well as operational analytics and performance features. In this context, Cloudflare processes in particular IP addresses, browser and device information, request and response metadata, usage data, and approximate location data derived from that information. Processing serves the technical provision, stability, security, and performance optimisation of the website and is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.

In addition, Cloudflare Web Analytics with Real User Measurements is enabled. According to our current configuration, RUM is configured to exclude visitor data from the EU; Cloudflare describes this feature as automatically dropping metrics from EU data centres. For visitors outside the EU, a cookie-free JavaScript beacon may collect page-view and performance metrics. No cookies and no described local browser storage are used by this service. Processing serves reach and performance analysis on the basis of Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR. According to the provider, related analytics values are currently accessible for up to six months.

Cookies and access to terminal equipment

According to the current configuration, we do not set our own cookies and do not use our own local browser storage such as localStorage, sessionStorage, IndexedDB or comparable storage for tracking or marketing purposes. According to the provider, Cloudflare Web Analytics does not use cookies or localStorage. Where technically necessary storage of or access to terminal equipment is required for transmitting the website, delivering requested content or security functions, we rely on the statutory exceptions under section 25(2) TDDDG. We do not use non-essential cookies or comparable terminal-device access without consent.

Contact by email

If you contact us by email, we process the data you provide, in particular your email address, name details, subject line, message content, and any other information you communicate to us, in order to handle your request and communicate with you.

Incoming emails sent to our domain are forwarded via Cloudflare Email Routing to a mailbox hosted with Gmail. They are stored there for handling. Outgoing emails are created through Gmail and delivered via SMTP2GO. The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR where your request is aimed at entering into or performing a contract, and otherwise Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR based on our legitimate interest in efficient business communication and handling inquiries.

We delete spam, misdirected, and obviously irrelevant emails promptly or after a short operational buffer period. Other communication data is generally stored until the respective matter has been concluded. Ordinary business correspondence that is not subject to statutory retention duties is then generally stored for a further three years from the end of the calendar year of the last relevant contact for documentation purposes and for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims. Emails subject to commercial, tax, or accounting retention duties are stored for the legally prescribed period, generally six or eight years from the end of the relevant calendar year. Where multiple retention grounds apply at the same time or data remains necessary for ongoing claims, audits, or disputes, the longer period applies.

Contact by telephone

If you contact us by telephone, we process your phone number and the information communicated during the call in order to handle your request. The telephone connection is handled through Telefónica Germany GmbH & Co. OHG (brand: Blau). The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR where the communication concerns pre-contractual or contractual matters, and otherwise Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR based on our legitimate interest in efficient business communication and handling inquiries. We do not record telephone calls.

DMARC management and email security

To protect our domain against spoofing and misuse, we use Cloudflare DMARC Management. In this context, Cloudflare processes aggregate DMARC reports, which may in particular contain technical transmission data and IP-related information about systems that send emails related to our domain. Processing serves abuse and fraud prevention and is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.

Recipients and transfers to third countries

Recipients of personal data in the processing operations described here include in particular GitHub, Cloudflare, Google, SMTP2GO, and our telecommunications provider.

In connection with hosting via GitHub Pages, GitHub also processes personal data in the United States and other countries. According to the provider’s publicly available information, GitHub relies on the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and on Standard Contractual Clauses for such transfers.

Cloudflare processes metadata for DNS, proxy, CDN, security, analytics, and email functions in Europe and in the United States. According to the provider’s publicly available information, Cloudflare relies on the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and on Standard Contractual Clauses for such transfers.

Google processes data in connection with Gmail in Ireland and globally, including in the United States. According to the provider’s publicly available information, Google relies on the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and on Standard Contractual Clauses for such transfers.

We use SMTP2GO in the configuration we use with an EU data centre in Amsterdam. According to the provider’s publicly available information, our telecommunications provider generally processes data in Germany and in the European Union.

Storage periods for technical data

The following storage logic applies in particular to infrastructure and communication services: GitHub-side security logs are processed for a provider-defined period. Cloudflare DNS Analytics provides up to eight days of history on the free plan. According to the provider, Cloudflare Web Analytics is accessible for up to six months where the service is active for the respective visitor. Cloudflare Email Routing provides routing analytics and activity data for up to 30 days. Cloudflare DMARC Management processes DMARC report data for a limited provider-defined evaluation period. For SMTP2GO, activity-data visibility on the free plan is limited to five days and cannot be extended. Separately, the provider describes default deletion of activity or email-header data after 35 days. We do not use SMTP2GO's optional Email Archiving for contents, attachments and delivery details. Deleted messages in Gmail generally remain in Trash for up to 30 days before being permanently deleted.

Provision of data

The provision of the technical data processed when accessing the website is technically necessary in order to deliver the website. If you contact us by email or telephone, providing your contact and communication data is voluntary. Without this data, however, we cannot handle your request or carry out the requested communication.

No automated decision-making

There is no automated decision-making, including profiling, within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR.
