Processing when you visit the website

When you access this website, certain information is processed for technical reasons that your browser transmits automatically. This may include, in particular: IP address, date and time of access, page/file accessed, amount of data transferred, referrer URL, browser type/version, operating system, and status/error messages.

The purposes of processing are the delivery of the website, ensuring security and stability (e.g., prevention of misuse and attacks), and statistical evaluation to improve our website.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interests), in particular our interest in providing the website without technical errors, maintaining security, and optimizing our web offering.

Hosting

This website is provided via GitHub Pages. In this context, GitHub processes technical access data; in particular, when a GitHub Pages site is visited, IP addresses are logged and stored for security purposes.

Provider: GitHub, Inc., 88 Colin P. Kelly Jr. St., San Francisco, CA 94107, USA
Further information: GitHub Privacy Statement

DNS/CDN/Proxy/WAF

Cloudflare

We use Cloudflare as our DNS provider and as CDN/proxy/WAF (Content Delivery Network / reverse proxy / web application firewall) to speed up delivery of the website and to protect it from attacks. This means that traffic between your browser and this website is routed via Cloudflare systems. In doing so, Cloudflare processes technical metadata (e.g., IP address and request data) to deliver the requested content and to ensure security.

Provider: Cloudflare, Inc., 101 Townsend Street, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA
Further information: Cloudflare Privacy Policy

Web analytics

Cloudflare Web Analytics

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics to evaluate usage of this website statistically. According to Cloudflare, Cloudflare Web Analytics does not use cookies or localStorage for analytics purposes and is not intended to identify individual visitors via fingerprinting techniques (e.g., IP address/user agent). Analytics results are typically provided in aggregated form (e.g., page views, rough origin by region/country, technical information such as browser type). This involves integrating a JavaScript beacon provided by Cloudflare into the website.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in measuring reach and optimizing our website).

Cookies and similar technologies

As of the current status, we do not use cookies for marketing purposes ourselves. However, third parties may use technically necessary cookies as part of technical protection and delivery functions (in particular via CDN/proxy/WAF) insofar as this is required to provide the website securely (e.g., to mitigate attacks or perform security checks, for example via technically necessary cookies such as __cf_bm).

The legal basis for the use of technically necessary cookies and for accessing information on your device is Section 25(2) of the German Telecommunications-Digital Services Data Protection Act (Telekommunikation-Digitale-Dienste-Datenschutz-Gesetz, TDDDG). To the extent that personal data is processed in this context, this is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in the secure and stable provision of the website).

Contacting us by email

If you contact us by email, we process the personal data you provide (e.g., name, email address, content of the message) in order to handle and respond to your inquiry.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in communication and handling inquiries).

Email providers: Cloudflare (Email Routing) and Google (Gmail). When handling emails, processing outside the EU/EEA, in particular in the United States, cannot be excluded.

Third-country transfers

Due to the service providers used, transfer of personal data to third countries outside the EU/EEA (in particular the United States) cannot be ruled out. For the United States, the European Commission has adopted an adequacy decision on the basis of the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (DPF) for appropriately certified organisations. In addition, appropriate safeguards may include, in particular, the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC). Even with safeguards in place, a residual risk (e.g., access by authorities) cannot be fully excluded.

Retention period

Server-related access data (server logs) is generally processed and retained for a limited period, which depends on the requirements and technical necessities of the hosting and infrastructure providers used, and is then deleted unless longer retention is required in individual cases for security reasons.

Inquiry data (email) is generally stored only as long as necessary to process the inquiry and is then deleted, unless statutory retention obligations apply.
